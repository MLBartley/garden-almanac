Dense planting strategy for Bozeman (Zone 5b, ~140 frost-free days): Oya pots irrigate ~4×4 ft each, so with 2 per bed you have full coverage if centered well. Use the square foot method but push plant counts 10–15% denser — the oya keeps moisture even during dry spells. Start transplants 4–6 weeks early indoors (Feb–Mar). Last frost ~May 15, first frost ~Sept 20.

Oya pot tip: Fill each oya before a hot stretch and check every 2–3 days in peak summer. At Bozeman's altitude (4,800 ft), evaporation is high and afternoons can be intense even when mornings are cool. In spring and fall, oya pots may stay full for a week. The clay seeps at a rate matched to soil tension — you mostly just need to top them off.
Your native restoration context: 5 years of sheet mulching + native bed installs is serious progress. The grass creep is normal — rhizomatous grasses like Kentucky bluegrass can push through decomposed cardboard after a few years. Below is a palette of natives well-suited to Bozeman (zone 5b, semi-arid, short season).
Penstemon strictus
Rocky Mountain Penstemon
Pollinator Drought tolerant Zone 4

Blue-purple spires June–July. Plant in clusters of 3–5 for visual impact. Spreads slowly by seed.

Echinacea purpurea
Purple Coneflower
Pollinator Bird seed Drought tolerant

Leave seed heads through winter for goldfinches. Self-seeds moderately. One of the best natives for Montana.

Ratibida columnifera
Prairie Coneflower
Pollinator Drought tolerant

Long bloom season (June–Sept). Extremely tough once established. Great for dry edges.

Gaillardia aristata
Blanket Flower
Pollinator Native to MT

Truly native to Montana. Fiery orange-red blooms all summer. Short-lived perennial, self-seeds freely.

Symphyotrichum laeve
Smooth Blue Aster
Late pollinator Fall bloom

Critical late-season nectar source (Aug–Oct). Pairs beautifully with goldenrod for fall structure.

Solidago canadensis
Canada Goldenrod
Pollinator Spreads

Can spread aggressively — plant where you want a mass. Excellent for late pollinators. Rhizomatous.

Linum lewisii
Prairie Flax
Pollinator Drought tolerant Native to MT

Sky-blue flowers, delicate texture. Direct sow in fall. Self-seeds reliably. Very tough.

Bouteloua gracilis
Blue Grama Grass
Native grass Drought tolerant

Native sod-forming grass — use to outcompete Kentucky bluegrass invaders. Low, tough, beautiful seed heads.

Grass creep strategy: Where bluegrass is returning, the most effective approach is smothering with new cardboard + 4" of wood chip mulch in fall, then planting aggressive native groundcovers in spring (try Phlox subulata or Thymus serpyllum as living mulch in high-creep zones). Avoid disturbing the soil — each tilling brings up new grass seeds.
