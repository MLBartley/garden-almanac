SEED STORAGE — ZONE 5B GUIDE
🏠 Cool / Dark  45–65°F
Pantry, basement, cool garage corner. 2–4 years viability. Garage is fine through the growing season but temperature swings reduce multi-year viability — not ideal for seeds you're keeping past next year.
🧊 Fridge  35–40°F
4–6 year viability. Seal in an airtight bag with a silica packet. Always let the sealed bag come to room temperature before opening — prevents condensation on the seeds.
❄️ Freezer  < 32°F, 5–10+ years
Seeds must be completely dry before freezing — moisture forms ice crystals that kill the embryo. Zip-lock + silica packet inside a sealed jar. Same warm-before-opening rule applies.
🌱 Winter Sowing  OUTDOOR METHOD
Sow in a covered clear container (milk jug cut and taped back) set outdoors — not a garage. The method requires real outdoor temperature cycling and natural light cues.

TIMING · Set out when outdoor temps are consistently below 40°F (Bozeman: late Oct–Nov). Seeds cold-stratify over winter and germinate naturally in spring when soil inside the container reaches 35–40°F — typically March–April.

COLD TOLERANCE · Container handles down to −20°F. Dormant seeds survive hard freezes; it's germinated seedlings in open soil that are vulnerable.

PLACEMENT · Face north-facing or in shade. South-facing catches too much February sun, warms the container prematurely, and triggers germination before the last frost — seedlings then get hit by a hard freeze with no roots established.
